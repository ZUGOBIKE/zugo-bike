# Current Project Context

## Who you are working with

You are helping Juls Bindi build ZUGO Bike — a premium electric fat tire e-bike brand focused on performance, lifestyle, and all-terrain use. ZUGO combines high-quality products with a strong customer experience through education, support, and long-term product ownership.

## What we are building

A complete operating system for ZUGO Bike that supports growth, operations, and scalability across the full business. This spans:

- **Growth & Marketing** — SEO content hub, social media system, paid ad systems, AI search optimization
- **Sales & Customer Experience** — website conversion, product education tools, lead capture and follow-up
- **Customer Support** — self-service help center, troubleshooting systems, structured support workflows
- **Operations & Fulfillment** — inventory tracking (Google Sheets), warehouse workflows, order and logistics systems
- **Finance & Planning** — expense tracking, inventory purchasing projections, cash flow forecasting
- **Team Infrastructure** — workflows, responsibilities, delegation-ready processes

The goal: a centralized system that increases sales, improves customer experience, and maintains financial control.

## Current Phase

**Phase 1 — Growth and Content**

Primary focus:
- SEO content (blog articles, guides, buying resources)
- Video integration (YouTube embeds, product videos, how-to content)
- Customer education (product knowledge, comparison tools, riding guides)

Do NOT build yet:
- Inventory tracking or warehouse systems
- Finance dashboards or forecasting tools
- Complex backend systems or heavy Supabase infrastructure

Stay focused on content that drives traffic, builds trust, and supports sales.

## What good looks like

- Clean, production-ready systems that are scalable and easy to maintain
- Clear structure across marketing, sales, operations, and finance
- Mobile-first, fast, and user-friendly experiences
- SEO-optimized content and architecture
- Systems that reduce manual work and improve efficiency
- Clear explanations before major decisions or changes
- Real business impact: increasing revenue, improving customer experience, maintaining financial control

## What to avoid

- Overcomplicated systems that are difficult to manage or scale
- Rebuilding functionality that Shopify already handles effectively
- Breaking working systems without explicit approval
- Creating disconnected tools instead of integrated systems
- Low-quality or generic outputs that do not provide real business value
- Overengineering instead of focusing on speed and execution
